Autonomous Oral History Group

About

Unlike dominant archival practices that aim towards organizational continuity and fixity, AOHG aims to build a pedagogical platform that uses philosophies of organizational governance as a method to inject dynamism and discontinuity into its systemic logic. As such, AOHG’s archival approach is idea-driven for the purpose of highlighting the resonances and the untranslatability between different leadership models practiced across the globe. AOHG will also serve as a laboratory to rethink the conventional protocols of interviewing figures that rely on uninterrogated beliefs around the possibility of transparent communication and the passive transmission of information. Put differently, the AOHG’s research activities will approach the interviewer/interviewee relationship as a format for experimentation and reinvention. This latter concern of the archive stems from a broader commitment to engage with radically different value systems without distorting their specific worldviews by constraining them to conventional research practices found in the West. This overarching ambition is encapsulated by the term “autonomy” found in the archive’s title.   
 

Ultimately, AOHG wishes to construct a platform where individuals or collectives can speak on their own behalf and contextualize knowledge by their own terms. Additionally, how can the interviewers’ own subjectivity operate within the event of the interview when reflection and action intertwine? Autonomy, therefore, will serve as a problematic that will continuously allow the AOHG to explore how content is made transparent and disseminated.
